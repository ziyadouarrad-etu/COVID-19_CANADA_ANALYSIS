### 1. Interpolation

**Goal**: Find interpolation polynomials for Infected($I$), Recovered($R$), and Deceased($D$) populations.


**Methods**: Lagrange Polynomials, Linear Splines, and Quadratic Splines.


**Analysis**: Comparative study illustrating the **Runge Phenomenon** in global interpolation versus the stability of local splines.



### 2. Solving Non-Linear Equations

**Objective**: Determine critical indicators for pandemic management .


* **Indicators**:

**Epidemic Peak**: Solving for  $\frac{dI}{dt} = 0$.


**Herd Immunity Threshold (HIT)**: Calculating the percentage of population required to stop the spread ($P_c = 1 - S_c$) .


**Critical Intervention Time ($t_c$)**: Identifying the moment hospital capacity ($I_{max}$) is exceeded using the **Bisection Method** .


**Reproduction Number ($R_0$)**: Estimating the basic reproduction rate from real data .





### 3. Numerical Differentiation
 
**Goal**: Use finite difference approximations to calculate variation rates.


* **Metrics**:
* Estimation of the time-varying transmission rate ($r$).


* Determination of initial exponential growth ($r_c$).


* Regional comparison of propagation velocity ($\frac{dI}{dt}$) .





### 4. Numerical Integration

**Application**: Estimating total cumulative infections ($I_{total} = \int \frac{dI}{dt} dt$) and calculating the necessary vaccine doses for herd immunity.



### 5. ODE Resolution

**Solvers**: Implementation of **Explicit and Implicit Euler** methods to solve the system of four coupled differential equations defining the SIRD dynamics .


**Validation**: Comparison of numerical simulations against recorded real-world data.
